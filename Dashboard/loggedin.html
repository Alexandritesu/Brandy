Our Blog

Some of the liquors that originated in the Philippines

Lambanog

A traditional Filipino liquor made from coconut sap, originated in pre-colonial times when early Filipinos fermented tuba,was later distilled into a stronger version during the Spanish colonial period. Today, it is celebrated globally for its unique coconut flavor, with modern variations preserving its cultural heritage while appealing to markets.

Don Papa Rum

Don Papa Rum originates from Negros Island in the Philippines, where it is crafted from the finest sugarcane molasses and aged in oak barrels. Launched in 2012, it draws inspiration from the local hero José "Papa" Esteban and has gained international acclaim for its smooth, rich flavor and connection to the island’s rum-making tradition.

Manille Liqueur de Calamansi

Manille Liqueur de Calamansi is a Filipino citrus-based liqueur made from the native calamansi fruit, blended with fine spirits and sugar for a refreshing, tangy flavor. Launched in 2008, it has gained both local and international popularity, offering a unique taste of the Philippines.
